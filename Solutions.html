Seamore's marine life identification model is built using Fathomnet and the Yolo framework, to accurately identify diverse marine life species. The model's precision contributes to comprehensive biodiversity monitoring and facilitates conservation efforts worldwide. Applications include ecological research, fisheries management, and the preservation of endangered species.

Seamore's trash detection model , leverages the YoloV8 framework, to swiftly and accurately detect and categorize oceanic debris. This powerful model plays a pivotal role in monitoring and mitigating marine pollution, enabling targeted clean-up initiatives and fostering sustainable practices for the protection of marine ecosystems worldwide. Its applications extend to environmental impact assessments, the implementation of ocean cleanup campaigns, and the promotion of a healthier and cleaner marine environment.

Seamore's Coral Reef Health Monitoring model leverages ResNet50 to precisely classify the health status of reefs, distinguishing between healthy and bleached conditions. Coral reefs are crucial as they support diverse marine life, protect coastlines, and contribute significantly to local economies through tourism and fisheries. However, these vital ecosystems face threats from climate change-induced phenomena like rising sea temperatures and ocean acidification. 

Seamore monitors crucial ocean parameters such as temperature, pH levels, and tide variations. By providing real-time data insights, seamore aids in the accurate prediction of climate patterns, facilitating informed decision-making for climate scientists, researchers, and policymakers. Applications include climate change analysis, oceanographic research, and predictive modeling for sustainable resource management.
